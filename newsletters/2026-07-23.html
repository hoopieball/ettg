🏭 Industry & Product News

Amplifon to Acquire GN Hearing in $2.64B Deal — Reshaping Global Audiology

The deal, expected to close by the end of 2026, will create a vertically integrated company combining hearing aid manufacturing with a global retail and service network. The combined entity will have revenues of approximately €3.3 billion, more than 20,000 employees, and operations in over 100 countries. Amplifon will retain the unique identity and strengths of GN Hearing, including its brands such as ReSound and Beltone, as well as its intellectual property, manufacturing operations, and existing network partnerships.

📎 Source: The Hearing Review

Orka O1 Pro Debuts as the World's First ANC Hearing Aid, Powered by Bose

Orka officially launched the Orka O1 Pro at the 2026 Appliance & Electronics World Expo (AWE) — the world's first Receiver-in-Canal (RIC) hearing aid to feature Powered by Bose QuietControl Active Noise Cancellation (ANC) technology. Bose QuietControl ANC technology neutralizes steady-state environmental noises before they reach the ear, while Bose SpeechClarity Voice technology precisely isolates and enhances human speech. Clinicians should note: real-world payoff likely depends on a low-latency implementation and strong SNR-improving algorithms — and without published human outcome data, its impact on speech understanding remains uncertain.

📎 Source: Hearing Tracker

Signia Launches Rechargeable CIC, Phonak Earns Dual Design Awards

Signia has announced U.S. market availability of its Insio Charge&Go CIC IX, a custom-made, rechargeable completely-in-canal (CIC) hearing aid — a form factor that has historically resisted rechargeability due to size constraints. Meanwhile, Phonak's Infinio Ultra Sphere and Virto R Infinio hearing aids have received the prestigious 2026 iF Design Award, which celebrates excellence in product design, innovation, functionality, and user experience. The Virto R Infinio has also separately received the 2026 Red Dot Award for Product Design.

📎 Source: Hearing Industries Association / AudiologyOnline

Lineage & William Demant Partner on ReSonance™ Auditory Cell Therapy

Lineage has partnered with William Demant Invest to advance ReSonance™ (ANP1), a cell therapy program targeting hearing loss. The three-year collaboration will focus on advancing Lineage's auditory neuronal cell transplant program, with preclinical development including cell manufacturing, proof-of-concept studies, translational and functional models, and delivery methods. The program represents one of the field's most closely watched bets on biological hearing restoration beyond conventional amplification.

📎 Source: Hearing Health & Technology Matters

Advanced Bionics Invests in Spiral Therapeutics for Next-Gen CI Drug Delivery

Advanced Bionics announced an equity investment and research collaboration with Spiral Therapeutics, a company focused on precision drug delivery for the inner ear — combining AB's three decades of CI expertise with Spiral's pioneering inner-ear delivery technology. The effort aims to explore how novel drug-delivery methods could support AB's long-term innovation roadmap and the broader future of cochlear implant technology. The collaboration signals growing interest in combining pharmacological and electrophysiological approaches within a single cochlear implant platform.

📎 Source: Advanced Bionics

🔬 Research & Clinical

PNAS Study: Serotonin-Auditory Circuit May Drive Tinnitus — SSRI Implications

Researchers at Oregon Health & Science University and Anhui University found that elevated serotonin levels in a mouse model resulted in elevated behavioral symptoms of tinnitus — the same neurotransmitter commonly leveraged to relieve depression and anxiety. By mapping a specific "straight-line" neural circuit from serotonin-producing neurons to the auditory system, researchers have explained why some patients report tinnitus flare-ups after starting SSRI medication. The authors suggest it may be possible to develop brain region-specific drugs that elevate serotonin selectively, separating antidepressant benefits from potentially harmful effects on hearing.

📎 Source: Medical Xpress / PNAS (2026)

APD Research Review: Standardization Gaps Persist, Cognitive Training Dominates

A new systematic review confirms that Auditory Processing Disorder manifests as impaired auditory information processing despite normal peripheral hearing — and that current clinical protocols lack standardization, hampering evidence-based intervention development. Among 156 studies analyzed, medical interventions were markedly underrepresented compared to rehabilitative approaches; the Random Gap Detection Test and Dichotic Digits Difference Test were most commonly used for diagnosis, while cognitive skill enhancement dominated intervention strategies (52.8%), followed by computer-based rehabilitation (26.4%). Publication frequency has shown consistent annual growth, peaking at 57 studies in 2024.

📎 Source: Journal of Clinical Medicine / PMC (2026)

Childhood APD: New Frontiers Study Challenges Diagnostic Validity

A newly published Frontiers in Human Neuroscience paper by dos Santos, Arruda, and Masruha delivers a critical appraisal of diagnostic validity, functional assessment, and interdisciplinary practice in childhood APD. A major source of uncertainty highlighted by the authors concerns the instruments used to diagnose APD — echoing longstanding calls in the field for consensus diagnostic frameworks. The diagnosis of APD remains difficult because of polymorphic symptoms possibly entangled with other difficulties such as learning, communication, and attention — and there is currently no gold standard in the literature for diagnosing APD.
