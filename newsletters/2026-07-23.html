Amplifon to Acquire GN Hearing in $2.6B Blockbuster Deal

Italy-based Amplifon, the world's largest hearing aid retailer, has signed a definitive agreement to acquire GN Store Nord's entire Hearing division — maker of ReSound and Beltone — in a transaction valued at approximately €2.3 billion. The combined entity is expected to operate across more than 100 countries with over 20,000 staff and more than 2,800 patents. The deal, expected to close by end of 2026, will create a vertically integrated company combining hearing aid manufacturing with a global retail and service network.

The €2.3 billion transaction would likely rank as the largest straight acquisition in hearing industry history, although smaller than the 2018 Sivantos-Widex merger by overall transaction value. Antitrust review and the formal carve-out of GN Hearing are still pending. Watch this space closely — this deal stands to reshape the competitive landscape for independent audiology practices and retail chains alike.

Orka O1 Pro: Bose-Powered Active Noise Cancellation Comes to Hearing Aids

Orka officially launched the Orka O1 Pro at the 2026 Appliance & Electronics World Expo (AWE), calling it the world's first Receiver-in-Canal (RIC) hearing aid to feature Bose QuietControl Active Noise Cancellation technology. Bose QuietControl ANC neutralizes steady-state environmental noise before it reaches the ear, while Bose SpeechClarity Voice technology isolates and enhances human speech simultaneously.

Clinicians should note that the real-world payoff likely depends on low-latency implementation and strong companion SNR-improving algorithms — without human outcome data, the device's impact on speech understanding in complex listening environments remains uncertain. At 3.5g per earpiece with 35+ hours of battery life, the form factor is compelling.

Cochlear's Nucleus Nexa: The "Smart" Implant That Updates Over Time

For decades, cochlear implants have delivered life-changing outcomes, but traditional implants have one inherent limitation: they are hardwired. Once implanted, their capabilities remain fixed even as technology advances. The Cochlear Nucleus Nexa System changes that paradigm. Cochlear Limited announced the system in a July 13, 2026 release, describing it as the world's first and only smart cochlear implant system.

The announcement signals a broader industry shift toward upgradeable, software-driven implantable devices — a model long familiar in cardiac rhythm management now arriving in hearing healthcare. Clinical details on upgrade pathways are expected as the product enters broader rollout.

MED-EL Founders Win 2026 Queen Elizabeth Prize — and Earn FDA First for Infant Implantation

MED-EL founders and cochlear implant pioneers Ingeborg and Erwin Hochmair have been awarded the 2026 Queen Elizabeth Prize for Engineering, recognising decades of groundbreaking innovation that transformed cochlear implants and restored hearing to millions worldwide. The honor is widely regarded as the engineering world's equivalent of the Nobel Prize.

Separately, MED-EL is now the first to offer an FDA-approved cochlear implant in the USA for children as young as seven months, enabling earlier access to hearing and speech development. The expansion of candidacy to younger infants may meaningfully shift early intervention protocols — audiologists managing pediatric referral pipelines should review their screening and counseling workflows accordingly.
